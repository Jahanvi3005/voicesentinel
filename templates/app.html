Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about VoiceSentinal and AI voice spoofing.

How does the Call Defense mode work?

Call Defense is a built-in module that monitors live system audio or your smartphone's microphone. You can run it continuously during a WhatsApp or Zoom call. If a deepfake crosses the 85% risk threshold, it instantly triggers a Red Alert and haptic vibrations.

What is Multimodal AI Intelligence?

Instead of just listening for robotic tones, VoiceSentinel combines acoustic fingerprinting (identifying AI audio generators via Wav2Vec2) with linguistic profiling (using OpenAI Whisper to transcribe text and detect scam intent like "urgent money transfers").

Will Standby Guardian work on my phone?

Yes! VoiceSentinel automatically uses the Screen Wake Lock API and Navigator Vibration API to keep your phone active while listening. If it detects a ringtone volume spike, your phone will vibrate to alert you to engage the Call Shield.

Is my analysis data secure?

Yes. For privacy and compliance, all raw audio feeds are processed in volatile memory and instantly destroyed. Only the final forensic diagnostic metadata is securely archived to your private MongoDB Atlas vault for historical tracking.

Important Setup & Instructions

How to configure VoiceSentinel to intercept and analyze live calls on your system.

Desktop Routing

  • 1. Install Cable: Mac users need BlackHole 2ch. Windows users need VB-Audio Cable.
  • 2. Set Output: Change your computer's System Sound Output to the virtual cable.
  • 3. Set Input: On this dashboard, allow microphone access and select the virtual cable as your input.

Mobile Speaker Mode

  • No Installation: Due to mobile OS privacy, apps cannot intercept live call audio directly.
  • Speaker Mode: Put your phone on speaker and place it near your device's microphone.
  • Standby Guardian: Enable this mode so VoiceSentinel wakes up and warns you upon hearing a ringtone.
